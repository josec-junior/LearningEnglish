# Do, Don't, Does and Doesn't (05/04/2026)

In English, we use the auxiliary verb (Do/Does) on the **Simple Present** to make questions, to deny questions or to short answers.

For `I/You/They`, we use `Do` (or `Don't` for negative sentences).

Example: Do they speak English? (Eles falam inglês?)

Example in the negative: No, I don't (Não, eu não [faço, falo, gosto, etc])

For `He/She/It`, we use `Does`. **When the auxiliary verb** (`Does` or `Doesn't`) **appears in the sentence, the main verb doesn't end with `s` or `es`**.

Example: What does Sarah prefer to drink? (O que Sarah prefere beber?)

Example in the negative: No, she doesn't like juice. (Não, ela não gosta de suco)

## Important!

For sentences on affirmative, we don't use the auxiliary verb.

Example: Sarah prefers to drink water. (Sarah prefere beber água)
