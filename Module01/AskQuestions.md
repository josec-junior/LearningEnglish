# Ask Questions (04/28/2026)

To ask most questions in English, we use this structure:

```plaintext
Question word + auxiliary verb + subject + main verb
```

Examples:

Where do you live? (Onde você mora?)

What do you like about it? (O que você gosta sobre isso?)

Do you live in America? (Você mora nos Estados Unidos?)

## Open Questions

For questions that are used to obtain more details, we use:

- Who (Quem?)
- Where (Onde?)
- When (Quando?)
- What (O quê?/Qual?)
- Why (Por quê?)
- How (Como?)
- How many/much? (Quanto?)

## Close Questions

For questions where the focus is "No" or "Yes", we use:

- Do (for present)
- Be (am or is or are)
- Can 
- Have

### Important!

When the **main verb** is the be (am/is/are), it doesn't need the auxiliary verb (do/does).

Example: Are you happy? (Você está feliz?)

If you are asking "who" is perfoming the action (and don't know who is the sbuject), also doesn't need the auxiliary verb.

Examplo: Who lives here? (Quem mora aqui?)
