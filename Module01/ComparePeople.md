# Compare People (04/30/2026)

## Comparing two people using "*but*" to contrast

This is used when you want to talk about two different ideas in the same sentence. Example:

**Question**: So you have two sisters, right? What do you think about them? (Então você tem duas irmãs, certo? O que você acha delas?)

They are both interesting, but totally different. (Elas são ambas interessantes, mas totalmente diferentes)

## Comparing two people using Verb To Be + "*more*"

This is used for long adjectives (with two or more syllables).  
Example: Elaine is a little bit shy. Luciana is more outgoing. (Elaine é um pouco introvertida. Luciana é mais extrovertida)

Another Example: Luciana is more outgoing than Elaine. (Luciana é mais extrovertida do que Elaine)

## Comparing two people using Adjective-ER + "*than*"

This rule is used for short adjectives (one syllable or **two syllables adjectives ending with -y**, like ***happy***).  
Example: Luciana is taller than Elaine. (Luciana é mais alta do que Elaine)

## Important!

For adjectives ending with CVC (consonant + vowel + consonant) like ***big*** or ***hot***, the last letter is replicated before the `-er` (Bigger, Hotter).

For adjectives ending with `-y` (like happy and funny), instead of `-er` at the ending, it's used `-ier`.
- Happy &rarr; Happier;
- Funny &rarr; Funnier.

# Vocabulary

- A little bit (Um pouco);
- Pretty (Bonita/Bonito) - Comumente utilizado para crianças;
- So-and-so (Fulano/Fulano de tal).
